About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Chemical Engineering at the University of Calgary. My research integrates materials synthesis, electrochemical analysis, electrolyte and electrode engineering, and advanced operando imaging to investigate interfacial phenomena and structure–performance relationships in electrochemical energy-storage systems.

My current work focuses on aqueous zinc–iodine redox flow batteries, operando visualization of electrolyte transport, polyiodide chemistry, magnetically recoverable nanofluid catalysts, and the development of customized electrochemical platforms for mechanistic studies.

Selected Research Projects

Operando Visualization of Redox Flow Batteries

Ongoing project

Developed an operando fluorescence confocal microscopy platform for real-time visualization of electrolyte transport and reaction progression in operating redox flow batteries.

  • Established quantitative imaging methods to map catalyst distribution and local electrolyte transport inside porous electrodes.
  • Integrated operando imaging with electrochemical measurements to correlate catalyst distribution, mass transfer, reaction kinetics, and battery performance.
  • Designed and fabricated customized electrochemical cells for spatially resolved characterization.

Organic Solvation Engineering for Aqueous Zinc–Iodine Flow Batteries

First-author manuscript in preparation

Developed an electrolyte-additive screening platform that combines electrochemical, spectroscopic, and full-cell analyses to investigate organic solvation effects in zinc–iodine flow batteries.

  • Identified DMF as an effective electrolyte additive for regulating iodine chemistry and battery performance.
  • Discovered a coordination-mediated phase transition governed by interactions between Zn²⁺, DMF, and polyiodide species.
  • Identified a spatiotemporal separation mechanism that maintains soluble iodine species near the electrode while promoting phase separation outside the reaction region.

Magnetically Recoverable Graphene Nanofluid for Zinc–Iodine Flow Batteries

First-author manuscript submitted

Developed Fe₃O₄-decorated graphene nanofluid catalysts that enhance iodine redox kinetics while enabling magnetic recovery and reuse of the catalytic particles.

  • Designed edge-functionalized graphene architectures with a high accessible catalytic surface area.
  • Improved the energy efficiency and kinetic performance of zinc–iodine flow batteries.
  • Established magnetic recovery, particle reuse, and system-level mass-balance methodologies.

Graphene–MXene Hybrid Electrodes for Supercapacitors

First-author paper published in ACS Materials Letters

Developed flexible N-doped graphene/Ti₃CNTx MXene hybrid electrodes through the synergistic integration of two-dimensional nanomaterials.

  • Achieved a volumetric capacitance of 331 F cm−3.
  • Maintained 93% capacitance retention after 10,000 charge–discharge cycles.
